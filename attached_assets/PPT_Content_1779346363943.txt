SLIDE 2 — PROBLEM STATEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  [TOP LEFT] — WHO IS IMPACTED
    • Executives — Upload stock data, initiate transactions
    • Managers / Approvers — Face approval overload and decision fatigue
    • Management Authorities — Risk compliance gaps and audit inconsistencies
    • Organizations at large — Bear financial and operational inefficiencies

  [TOP RIGHT] — CURRENT PAIN POINTS
    • Manual Dependency: Continuous human involvement for every validation,
      reconciliation, and approval step
    • Delayed Decisions: Approval bottlenecks slow stock movement and operations
    • Poor Fault Resilience: Partial uploads, missed transactions, and silent
      failures go undetected
    • Lack of Intelligence: Systems capture data but do not reason or learn from it

  [BOTTOM LEFT] — WHY IT MATTERS
    Scale:   Inventory spans departments, locations, and thousands of daily
             transactions — too large for purely manual management.
    Urgency: Manual reconciliation and delayed approvals directly impact
             business continuity and SLAs.
    If left unaddressed:
      → Stockouts or overstocking
      → Audit and compliance failures
      → Rising operational costs
      → Eroded trust in inventory data

  [BOTTOM RIGHT / CENTRE] — THE PROBLEM
    Modern inventory systems lack autonomous intelligence.
    They capture transactions but cannot self-validate, detect anomalies
    early, or self-correct without human intervention — leading to
    persistent data drift, approval delays, and operational risk at scale.

SLIDE 3 — PROPOSED SOLUTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  [HEADLINE — Proposed Solution]
    Mavericks Inventory is an AI-augmented autonomous inventory intelligence
    platform combining enterprise-grade controls (maker–checker, RBAC, full
    audit trails) with Generative AI-driven insights, anomaly detection, and
    self-monitoring workflows. Humans intervene only when AI flags risk or
    deviation — ensuring persistent accuracy, resilience, and sustainability.

  Key Feature 1 — Autonomous Inventory Validation
    Continuously validates stock levels using historical patterns and
    rule-based checks to proactively prevent data drift and discrepancies.

  Key Feature 2 — AI-Driven Anomaly Detection
    Detects unusual stock movements, spikes, or inconsistencies in real time
    with risk scoring and plain-language explanations for every alert.

  Key Feature 3 — Intelligent Approval Assist
    Provides AI-generated approve / review / reject recommendations with
    contextual reasoning, helping approvers decide faster and with confidence.

  Key Feature 4 — Fault-Tolerant & Self-Healing Workflows
    Automatically detects partial uploads, failed transactions, and system
    issues, triggering rollback and recovery mechanisms without manual effort.

  [HEADER NOTE]
    Gen AI is used surgically — only where contextual reasoning adds value
    that rule-based logic cannot provide. This keeps costs low and outputs trustworthy.

  Use Case 1 — Inventory Insight Generator
    Input:       Current stock levels + historical distribution data
    Output:      Natural-language inventory health summary for managers
    Enhancement: Eliminates manual analysis; gives instant, readable stock
                 status without requiring BI skills

  Use Case 2 — Approval Recommendation Engine
    Input:       Distribution request details + historical demand trends
    Output:      Approve / Review / Reject recommendation with reasoning
    Enhancement: Reduces approval turnaround time and combats decision fatigue

  Use Case 3 — Anomaly Explanation Assistant
    Input:       Detected anomaly event + relevant historical context
    Output:      Plain-language explanation of the anomaly cause + suggested
                 corrective action
    Enhancement: Builds trust in AI alerts and dramatically reduces
                 manual investigation effort

  [WHAT MAKES IT UNIQUE]
    Unlike traditional inventory tools that are purely transactional, Mavericks
    Inventory introduces autonomous intelligence — the system reasons, explains,
    and self-corrects. It is the first inventory platform built around an
    exception-driven human model.

  1 — Autonomous Inventory Intelligence
      A self-monitoring, self-correcting system that continuously validates stock
      accuracy without waiting for human intervention.

  2 — Exception-Driven Human Involvement
      Humans are involved only when AI detects meaningful deviations — not for
      every routine operation — drastically reducing cognitive load.

  3 — Explainable AI at Every Step
      Every AI output (recommendation, anomaly alert, insight) comes with a
      plain-language rationale — fully interpretable and auditable.

  4 — Sustainable & Cost-Conscious AI Usage
      Gen AI is triggered only on exceptions (anomalies, approvals, insights).
      Standard workflows run on deterministic logic — keeping API costs minimal.

SLIDE 10 — GEN AI DESIGN STRATEGY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  1 — Rationale for Gen AI
      Traditional rule-based logic can flag anomalies but cannot explain them
      or recommend nuanced actions. Gen AI (GPT-4o via Azure OpenAI) provides
      contextual reasoning, plain-language insights, and adaptive recommendations
      that no static rule set can replicate — while remaining fully auditable.

  2 — Prompt Design
      All prompts use a structured template:
        • Context injection — limited relevant historical data (last N records)
        • Clear role framing — "You are an inventory analyst..."
        • Hard output format constraints — JSON schema enforced
        • Explicit boundaries — no assumptions beyond provided data
      This ensures deterministic, low-hallucination responses.

  3 — AI Workflow
      Gen AI is triggered at three precise points only:
        → Anomaly detected: AI explains cause + recommends action
        → Approval review: AI scores risk + recommends decision
        → Dashboard load: AI generates inventory health summary
      All other flows use standard business logic — no AI overhead.

  4 — Human-in-the-Loop
      AI never takes autonomous action. All recommendations require human
      confirmation. Managers can override any AI suggestion with mandatory
      remarks. Fallback rule-based logic activates if AI is unavailable.
      Full audit trail maintained for every AI-assisted decision.

SLIDE 11 — COST AWARENESS & AI USAGE OPTIMISATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  [THREE PILLARS]

  Prompt Efficiency
    • Structured prompts with strict token budgets
    • Only relevant context injected (not full history)
    • JSON-formatted outputs to avoid verbose generation
    • Consistent prompt templates reused across use cases

  Limited API Calls
    • AI invoked only on exceptions — not on every transaction
    • Batch insight generation (one call per dashboard load)
    • No real-time streaming; async calls with caching
    • Rate-limit guards and retry logic in place

  Rule-Based Fallbacks
    • Standard approval flows use deterministic business rules
    • AI assists but never blocks workflow execution
    • If Azure OpenAI is unavailable, rule-based fallback activates seamlessly
    • Cached AI summaries reused within short TTL windows

SLIDE 12 — RISKS, ASSUMPTIONS & CONSTRAINTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  Scope Boundaries (what is OUT of scope)
    • No financial accounting or invoicing
    • No vendor procurement or purchase order workflows
    • No IoT integrations or warehouse automation
    • No GST / statutory compliance reporting

  Key Risks
    • Incorrect Excel uploads causing invalid stock data
      → Mitigation: Template validation + downloadable error report
    • AI misinterpretation of edge-case anomalies
      → Mitigation: Human-in-the-loop confirmation; fallback rules
    • Approval bottleneck if approvers are unavailable
      → Mitigation: Configurable escalation mechanism

  Assumptions
    • Excel templates will be standardized and finalized before rollout
    • Approval hierarchy is configurable per business unit needs
    • All users have stable internet access during operations

  Technical Constraints
    • Azure OpenAI API rate limits apply (managed with retry + caching)
    • Limited historical data available in early stages — AI context window
      may be smaller until data accumulates
